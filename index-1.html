"They didn’t just brew tea. They brewed a movement."
In a dim Kyoto alley once untouched by tourists, whispers began — of a matcha so smooth,
so bold, it didn’t taste like health… it tasted like power. That was Matcha Made.
Founded in 2019 by a burned-out creative and a runaway chef, Matcha Made wasn’t born for cafes.
It was built in resistance to caffeine chaos and sugar-coated wellness. No syrups. No sprinkles.
Just ceremonial-grade rebellion in a cup. They didn’t market. They murmured. QR codes hidden in zines.
From underground art shows to sleepless coder dens in Berlin, Matcha Made became a signal. Of taste. Of control.
Of quiet power. Today, the whisk is their weapon. The cup is their canvas. And calm? That’s just the start.
